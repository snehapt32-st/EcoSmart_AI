What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. Since the 1800s, human industrial activities have been the primary driver of climate change, mainly due to burning fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas, releasing heat-trapping greenhouse gases.

🚨

Why It Matters

Rising global temperatures lead to severe droughts, water scarcity, catastrophic wildfires, rising sea levels, flooding, melting polar ice, catastrophic storms, and declining biodiversity. This threatens agricultural food chains and human habitats worldwide.

♻️

The Solution Strategy

Combating climate change requires shifting power generation to renewable energies (like wind and solar), adopting electric mass public transit, implementing circular economy practices to remove solid waste landfill build-ups, and protecting and expanding green forests.

13

Sustainable Development Goal 13: Climate Action

Formulated by the United Nations, Goal 13 calls on countries globally to strengthen adaptive resilience to natural climate hazards, integrate climate strategies directly into national policies, and dramatically improve institutional education, human capacities, and early warnings.
